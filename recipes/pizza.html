Quatro Queijos Pizza

Description

Quatro Queijos, or "Four Cheese" pizza, is a celebrated staple of Italian-inspired cuisine, known for its rich, decadent profile and harmonious blend of textures. While the specific selection of cheeses can vary by region or chef, the traditional composition typically features a balanced mix of creamy, salty, and pungent varieties. In Brazil, this often results in a combination of mozzarella for stretch, parmesan for a sharp salty finish, provolone for a touch of smokiness, and either gorgonzola or catupiry to provide a distinct creaminess that ties the flavors together.
The sensory experience of this pizza is defined by the way the different fats and proteins interact under high heat. As it bakes, the mozzarella creates a golden, bubbling foundation, while the more intense cheeses like gorgonzola melt into the crevices, offering sharp bursts of flavor. The result is a complex, savory palette that ranges from the mild, lactic notes of the base to the sophisticated, bold punch of the aged inclusions. Often finished with a sprinkle of dried oregano or a drizzle of olive oil, it remains a favorite for those who appreciate the depth and versatility of dairy.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (wheat flour, water, yeast, salt, and olive oil)
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Provolone cheese
  • Gorgonzola cheese (or Catupiry)
  • Dried oregano
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough: Stretch or roll out your pizza dough on a floured surface until it reaches your desired thickness. If you are using a pizza stone, preheat it in the oven at its highest temperature for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Apply the Base: Spread a thin, even layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving about 2 cm around the edges for the crust.
  3. Layer the Cheeses: Distribute the mozzarella evenly as the first layer to ensure a consistent melt. Follow with the provolone and parmesan. Drop small dollops of gorgonzola or catupiry across the surface to create pockets of intense flavor.
  4. Bake the Pizza: Place the pizza in the oven. Bake until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling and starting to develop golden spots. In a standard home oven at 250Â°C, this usually takes 10 to 15 minutes.
  5. Finishing Touches: Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately sprinkle with dried oregano. Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the top and add fresh basil leaves if desired.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the pizza rest for two minutes to allow the cheeses to set slightly before slicing.
