Tilápia à Milanesa

Description

Tilápia à Milanesa is a quintessential seafood dish in Brazil, favored for its light, flaky texture and the satisfying crunch of its golden-brown breading. Tilapia is an ideal choice for this preparation due to its mild flavor, which carries seasonings well without being overly "fishy." The fillets are typically marinated in a mixture of lime juice, garlic, salt, and pepper, then dredged through flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs to create a protective shell that locks in moisture during the cooking process.
The result is a versatile protein that serves as a cornerstone of the Brazilian "Prato Feito" or as a popular "tira-gosto" in lakeside kiosks and bars. When fried to perfection, the exterior provides a crisp, savory contrast to the tender, white meat inside. It is traditionally served with a side of white rice, brown beans, and a fresh salad, often accompanied by lime wedges to add a bright, acidic zest that cuts through the richness of the fried coating.

Ingredients

  • Tilapia fillets
  • Lime or lemon juice
  • Minced garlic
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • All-purpose flour (for the first coating)
  • Beaten eggs
  • Breadcrumbs or Panko (for the final crunch)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. Season the Fish: Place the tilapia fillets in a bowl and season them with lime juice, minced garlic, salt, and black pepper. Let them marinate for at least 15 to 30 minutes so the meat absorbs the flavors.
  2. Prepare the Breading Station: Set up three shallow bowls. Put all-purpose flour in the first, beaten eggs with a pinch of salt in the second, and breadcrumbs (or Panko for extra crunch) in the third.
  3. Dredge in Flour: Take a fillet and coat it lightly in the flour, shaking off any excess. This helps the egg stick to the fish.
  4. Dip in Egg: Submerge the floured fillet into the beaten eggs until it is completely covered. Coat with Breadcrumbs: Place the fillet into the breadcrumbs, pressing firmly on both sides to ensure the coating is even and secure.
  5. Fry until Golden: Heat enough vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully place the fillets in the hot oil and fry for about 3 to 4 minutes per side until they are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drain and Serve: Remove the fillets from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess fat. Serve immediately with fresh lime wedges.
